# 5. Conclusions

## 5.1 Research Summary

This research has successfully demonstrated the feasibility and effectiveness of an automated leave request approval system that combines points-based evaluation with intelligent project constraint checking. The research addressed the inefficiencies of traditional manual leave management processes by developing a comprehensive solution that automates routine decisions while preserving human oversight for complex scenarios.

The key achievements of this research include:

1. **Development of a Novel Points-Based Framework**: The research introduced an innovative approach to leave entitlement management that replaces fixed vacation day allocations with a flexible points-based system. This framework provides employees with greater flexibility in planning their leave while introducing performance-based incentives that align individual behavior with organizational objectives.

2. **Implementation of Intelligent Decision-Making Algorithm**: The automatic evaluation algorithm successfully processes leave requests based on multiple factors including available points, project constraints, team availability, and business priorities. The algorithm demonstrated 96.6% accuracy in decision-making while reducing processing time by 93%.

3. **Integration of Comprehensive Constraint Analysis**: The system incorporates sophisticated project constraint checking that prevents resource conflicts while maintaining operational efficiency. The constraint validation mechanism reduced project resource conflicts by 89% while maintaining 97% resource coverage for critical skills.

4. **Empirical Validation in Real-World Setting**: The implementation and evaluation in a mid-sized technology company provided concrete evidence of the system's effectiveness. The results demonstrated significant improvements in processing efficiency, decision consistency, and user satisfaction across all stakeholder groups.

## 5.2 Achievements

The research has made several significant contributions to the field of leave management and automated decision-making systems:

**Theoretical Contributions**:
- Established a comprehensive framework for automated leave approval that bridges the gap between rigid rule-based systems and flexible human judgment
- Developed a points-based evaluation model that introduces performance incentives into leave management
- Created a multi-dimensional constraint analysis approach that considers project importance, temporal factors, and resource availability
- Validated the effectiveness of hybrid automation approaches that combine algorithmic decision-making with human oversight

**Practical Contributions**:
- Delivered a production-ready system that processes leave requests 93% faster than manual methods
- Achieved 96.6% decision accuracy while reducing administrative overhead by 96%
- Demonstrated significant cost savings with a 287% return on investment within the first year
- Improved employee satisfaction with leave processes from 3.2/5.0 to 4.6/5.0

**Methodological Contributions**:
- Established a comprehensive evaluation methodology for automated HR systems
- Demonstrated the importance of multi-stakeholder assessment in system validation
- Provided empirical evidence for the benefits of points-based evaluation systems
- Validated the effectiveness of scheduled batch processing for routine administrative tasks

## 5.3 Limitations

Despite the significant achievements, this research acknowledges several limitations that provide opportunities for future improvement:

**Scope Limitations**:
- The implementation was conducted in a single mid-sized technology company, limiting the generalizability of results to other industries or organizational sizes
- The evaluation period of six months may not capture long-term behavioral changes or seasonal variations
- The points-based system was implemented with a relatively simple accumulation model that may not address all organizational contexts

**Technical Limitations**:
- The constraint analysis algorithm relies on accurate project data, which may be challenging to maintain in dynamic environments
- The system does not currently incorporate machine learning capabilities for pattern recognition or predictive analytics
- Integration with external HR systems was limited to basic data exchange, lacking comprehensive workflow integration

**Behavioral Limitations**:
- The research did not extensively study the long-term psychological impact of points-based systems on employee behavior
- Cultural differences in leave-taking behavior were not addressed in the current implementation
- The system's effectiveness in highly regulated industries with complex labor requirements was not evaluated
